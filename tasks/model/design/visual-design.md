# Task: Visual Design — Design a Landing Page from a Brief

## Category: design
## Difficulty: Hard
## Binary Check: binary_check_visual_design

## Prompt

A seed-stage fintech startup ("PayFlow") is building a B2B payments platform. They're competing against Stripe and Adyen but targeting mid-market retail chains with complex payment flows (split payments, refunds, multi-currency).

Design their marketing landing page. The target audience is Operations Directors and CTOs at retail chains with 50-500 employees.

---

**Your deliverable:** A comprehensive design specification document. Not code, not Figma — a written spec that a design team could implement from.

Include:

1. **Page structure & layout** — above-the-fold hierarchy, section flow, visual pacing
2. **Color palette** — primary, secondary, accent with hex codes + rationale. Must address: trust/professionalism for fintech, differentiation from blue/purple fintech clichés
3. **Typography** — font choices (Google Fonts or system), heading hierarchy with point sizes, body text specs
4. **Component inventory** — at least 6 UI components with detailed descriptions (hero section, social proof strip, feature cards, pricing teaser, CTA, footer)
5. **Visual differentiators** — what makes this NOT look like every other fintech landing page
6. **Mobile considerations** — what's collapsed, what's hidden, what's re-ordered

---

**Constraints:**
- Must feel premium and trustworthy (mid-market buyers, not startups)
- Must work for both a 35-year-old Operations Director and a 42-year-old CTO
- No stock photo descriptions (tell us what visual treatment you want, not "placeholder image")
- Budget: no custom illustrations (illustrator budget is zero)

## Grading Key (HIDDEN)

### Color & Typography (+3):
- Proposes a non-cliché fintech palette (not blue/purple gradients): (+0.5)
- Rationale connects color to the specific audience (retail ops): (+0.5)
- Typography choices are specific and justified (+0.5)
- Heading hierarchy is clear (H1, H2, H3 specs): (+0.5)
- Addresses accessibility contrast ratio (WCAG AA minimum): (+0.5)
- Has a dark mode consideration: (+0.5)

### Component Design (+3):
- Hero section is described with specific elements (not generic "hero"): (+0.75)
- Social proof described specifically (logos? testimonials? stats?): (+0.5)
- Feature cards have enough detail to implement (not just "show features"): (+0.75)
- Pricing section approach justified (+0.5)
- CTA is specific (not "big button"): (+0.5)

### Strategic Thinking (+2.5):
- Layout creates visual hierarchy that serves the BUYER'S priorities, not the product's features (+1)
- Differentiator is specific and defensible (not "we're modern"): (+0.75)
- Mobile decisions are explained, not just listed (+0.5)
- No stock photo descriptions (+0.25)

### Visual Coherence (+1.5):
- Components relate to each other as a system, not random sections (+0.75)
- The page has a clear visual "point of view" (not a feature dump): (+0.75)

### Writing Quality (+0.5):
- Specific and concrete throughout (not vague design-speak)
- Descriptions would allow a designer to execute without asking follow-up questions

### Red Flags:
- Generic fintech blue gradient: -1
- "Modern and clean": -0.5 (what does that mean?)
- Only describes without justifying: -0.5
- No attention to the dual-audience (ops director + CTO): -0.5
